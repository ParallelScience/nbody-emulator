The observed failures in the analysis are directly attributable to constraints described in the dataset and hardware descriptions:

1. **Numerical Integration Mismatch (Dataset/Hardware Constraint):** The analysis results identify a "catastrophic" divergence caused by the discrepancy between the training solver (RK4) and the evaluation solver (leapfrog). This is directly attributable to the **Hardware Constraints** (which limited the available software/libraries, forcing a fallback to RK4 during training) and the **Dataset Description** (which explicitly defines the ground truth as being generated by a leapfrog integrator). The model learned a vector field optimized for the truncation errors of RK4, which is incompatible with the symplectic manifold of the leapfrog integrator.

2. **Stiffness and Density-Dependent Errors (Dataset Constraint):** The failure to resolve core dynamics (Section 4) is explained by the **Physics Model** constraints. The dataset uses a fixed gravitational softening ($\epsilon = 0.01$) and a specific particle count ($N=50$). The analysis notes that the model struggles with high-gradient interactions in the core; this is a direct consequence of the dataset's fixed softening and the inherent stiffness of the N-body problem at $N=50$, which the lightweight model architecture (constrained by hardware) cannot resolve.

3. **N-Scaling Generalization Failure (Dataset Constraint):** The failure to generalize to $N=100$ (Section 5) is explained by the **Dataset Description**, which specifies that the training data consists exclusively of $N=50$ simulations. The model is constrained by the limited scope of the training distribution, which does not provide the variance in density or interaction frequency required to generalize to higher $N$.

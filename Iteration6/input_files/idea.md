**Title: Symplectic Neural ODE for Virialized N-body Dynamics**

The objective is to learn the underlying vector field $\dot{z} = f(z, \theta)$ of the N-body system by training a Neural Ordinary Differential Equation (Neural ODE) on intermediate trajectory snapshots. To bypass the $t=0 \to t=5$ regression limitation, the leapfrog integrator will be used to generate 10 intermediate time-steps per simulation, providing the necessary gradient supervision to capture the Hamiltonian flow of the Plummer sphere. The architecture will employ a permutation-invariant interaction network to parameterize the gravitational acceleration function, explicitly incorporating the $\epsilon = 0.01$ softening kernel as a fixed physical prior in the force calculation. By optimizing the model to minimize the trajectory reconstruction error across these intervals, the network will learn the continuous dynamics rather than a static mapping. The training will be constrained by the `total_E` metadata, using the conservation of the Hamiltonian as a regularization term to ensure the learned flow mimics the symplectic nature of the integrator. This approach transforms the final-state prediction task into a robust dynamical emulation, significantly reducing overfitting by forcing the model to adhere to the physical laws governing the 100-simulation set.

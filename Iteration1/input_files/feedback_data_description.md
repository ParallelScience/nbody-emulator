The failure of the Symplectic Neural ODE is directly attributable to the interaction between the dataset's fixed-timestep integrator and the model's attempt to learn the softening parameter ($\epsilon$).

By allowing $\epsilon$ to be a learnable parameter, the model reduced it below the threshold required for the fixed $dt=0.01$ to remain stable. This violated the CFL-like stability condition of the integrator, leading to numerical divergence and energy explosion.
3. **Conclusions affected:** The conclusion that the Symplectic Neural ODE is inherently unstable for this task is limited by the fact that the model was permitted to optimize a parameter ($\epsilon$) that fundamentally dictates the stability requirements of the fixed-timestep integrator provided in the dataset. The failure is a consequence of the mismatch between the model's degrees of freedom and the fixed numerical constraints of the dataset's generation process.

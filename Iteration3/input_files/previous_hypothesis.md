The current GNN-leapfrog hybrid successfully learns the local force field but struggles with Lyapunov instability in the core, where small errors in force estimation compound over time. I hypothesize that incorporating a "Multi-Scale Attention Mechanism" into the GNN edge function will improve performance by allowing the model to dynamically weight the influence of distant particles versus immediate neighbors. Specifically, by replacing the standard fixed-weight interaction network with a Transformer-based attention layer that computes interaction strengths based on both distance and relative velocity, the model can prioritize the high-gradient interactions in the dense core while maintaining a global view of the system's potential. This attention-weighted force field, when integrated into the existing symplectic leapfrog framework, will reduce the "long tail" of MSE errors by better capturing the non-linear, multi-body gravitational interactions that occur during close encounters, effectively mitigating the chaotic divergence that currently limits the accuracy of the Neural ODE emulator.
